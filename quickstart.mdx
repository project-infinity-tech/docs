## Step 1 — Go to the Scram Projects Dashboard

Open the link below in your browser.

[https://workspace.buildwithscram.com](https://workspace.buildwithscram.com/)

---

## Step 2 — Sign in or create your account

You'll see the Scram sign-in screen. 

![Scram Sign In screen](/images/step2.png)

Sign in with a Google Account. 

You'll receive a confirmation email with a code — enter it when prompted to verify your address.

---

## Step 3 — Tell Scram what to build

You're now on the **Projects Dashboard**, and the big prompt at the top — **"What shall we build today?"** — is the fastest way to start. Describe your project in your own words and press the arrow. Scram creates a project for you and starts planning your app straight away.

![Projects Dashboard prompt](/images/step3-dashboard-prompt.PNG)

Scram will give your project a name based on your prompt. You can change this any time by double-clicking the name.

<Info>
  It may take a few seconds for your project to be created — just be patient and you will be taken to your new project!
</Info>

Prefer to set things up first? You can also click **Create new project** to start with an empty project and describe what you want in the chat once you're inside.

---

## Step 4 — Answer Scram's questions and approve the Plan

Inside your project, Scram may ask a few questions to understand what you're after. Answer them in the chat, then review and approve the **Plan** it comes up with. That's it — Scram will get to work!

![Scram chat input](/images/step5a.png)

<Info>
  Don't be alarmed if you see Errors or Warnings during the build — Scram handles these automatically.
</Info>

---

## Step 5 — In the future

Next time, you can start a brand-new idea from the dashboard prompt, or click an existing project in the projects list to carry on where you left off.

<Info>
  If you run into any issues, use the **Report a Bug** button in the top right corner of the screen.
</Info>

---

## Step 6 — Run your app

Once Scram is done, switch from **Edit** to **Run** mode at the top of the editor. Your app starts right there — no new tab, no waiting.

![Edit/Run mode toggle](/images/step6-run-toggle.PNG)

This isn't a preview or a mock-up — it's your real app, fully working. Add records, click around, try to break it. Switch back to **Edit** at any time; flipping between the two is instant.

<Note>
  Used Scram before? The **Launch** button is gone — **Run** mode replaces it for testing, and **Publish** replaces it for going live.
</Note>

Here is an example of what your app might look like. The dropdowns and buttons will all work right in the editor.

![Working app example](/images/step6b.png)

---

## Step 7 — Make changes

You don't need to stop using your app to change it. While in **Run** mode, just type into the chat — ask Scram to make changes or fix anything you've spotted, and it builds them in the background while you carry on testing.<br /><br />You can of course use **Edit** mode and make any changes manually. No need to always use the AI. That is what makes Scram different to many other tools.

---

## Step 8 — Share your work in progress

Want to show someone your app without them looking over your shoulder? Click the **external link icon** to get a shareable link to your app.

{/*  SCREENSHOT NEEDED: external link icon in the editor  */}

![External link icon](/images/step8-external-link.PNG)

This is your app's **development link** — perfect for sending to a colleague for feedback. It is _not_ your live, published app. Anything you or your testers do here (including any data entered) stays in your development environment.

---

## Step 9 — Publish when you're ready

When your app is ready for the real world, click **Publish**. This takes you to the publish page, where your app goes live at its own separate address.

The development link and the live link are different apps in every way that matters:

|  | Development link | Live link (Published) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Address** | Your app's dev URL from the editor | Your app's own live URL |
| **Audience** | You and anyone you share it with | Your real users |
| **Data** | Development database — safe to experiment | Live database — completely separate |

Data never crosses between the two: records you create while testing in Run mode or via the development link will not appear in your published app, and vice versa.
