## What is Scram?

Scram is a **full-stack app builder**. You describe what you want to build — in plain language, to an AI — and Scram builds it: the pages, the database, the logic, the design, all of it. The result is a real, production-quality web application, not a prototype or a mockup.

Unlike no-code tools that give you templates to fill in, Scram builds from your description. Unlike hiring a developer, it builds in seconds. And unlike most AI tools, what it produces is not throwaway code — it is a structured, maintainable application you can continue to evolve.

## How do I start? What Can I Do Right Away?

Open the AI assistant and describe what you want to build. Not a technical specification — just the idea, in plain English:

> *"I need a CRM where my team can track leads and log call notes"*

> *"Build me a booking system for my photography studio"*

> *"Create an internal dashboard showing our weekly sales figures"*

Scram will ask a few clarifying questions and then start building. Within minutes you will have pages, a database, working navigation, and real data flowing through the app.

You do not need to set anything up first. No database configuration, no hosting decisions, no framework choices. Just describe and build.

---

## How Do I Add Content?

There are two ways, and you can use both freely:

**Tell the AI what you want** — *"Add a page where users can view their invoices"* or *"Add a profile photo to the user card"* — the AI makes the change directly. This is the fastest approach for structural additions.

**Edit visually** — click on any element on the page to select it. Change its text, adjust its styling, move it, resize it. The visual editor gives you direct control over every component on every page.

For **data** — the actual content your app stores and displays — you add that through the app itself, just as your users would. Create a product, submit a form, add a record.

## How Long Will It Take to See Something Live?

You can see something working in minutes. The preview is live as you build — open it in a browser tab, and it updates as changes are made. There is no compile step, no deployment pipeline, no waiting.

When you are ready to share it with real users, publishing is a single action. From the first conversation to a live URL that someone else can visit, for most apps, is well under an hour.

## Will It Look Good If I'm Not a Designer?

Yes — and here is why.

Scram's AI builds with professional design standards by default: consistent spacing, typography, colour hierarchy, and layout. It does not produce the generic clip-art aesthetic of older no-code tools. The starting point is already clean and considered.

**To make it look even better:**

- **Describe a visual direction** — *"Make it feel premium and minimal"* or *"I want it to feel friendly and approachable"* — the AI will adjust the theme, colours, and component styles accordingly.
- **Choose a colour palette** — tell the AI your brand colours and it will apply them consistently across the entire app through the theme system.
- **Ask for specific changes** — *"Make the header darker"*, *"use rounded cards throughout"*, *"increase the spacing so it feels more spacious"* — every visual detail is adjustable in plain language.

You do not need to know what a font stack is or how CSS works. Describing the feeling you want is enough.

## What Happens If I Make a Mistake?

Nothing is permanent, and nothing is fragile.

- **The AI can fix it** — describe what went wrong or what you want instead, and it will correct it. *"The button is in the wrong place"*, *"that colour is too harsh"*, *"the form is not saving correctly"* — all fixable in a single message.
- **You can edit directly** — click on anything and change it. Every property of every component is editable.
- **The structure is transparent** — Scram is not a black box. You can see the pages, the database, the workflows, the components. Nothing is hidden from you.
- **Support is available** — if something is genuinely broken or confusing, Scram's support team can help. You are not alone with a cryptic error message and a blank search bar.

The worst realistic outcome of most mistakes is that something looks wrong or behaves unexpectedly. Neither of those is permanent, and neither requires technical knowledge to fix.
